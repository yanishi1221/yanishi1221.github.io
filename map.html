Unbalanced Economy Performance and Education Expenditure

In China, the economic performances differ from provinces to provinces. The GDP/Capita provides a basic measure of the value of output per person.
The volume of education expenditure is directly correlated with the economic prowess of the region.
The education expenditure is unbalanced distributed between urban centres and the least developed regions. This applies both in the sense of the distribution of the national budget across regions, and also in terms of different regions’ varying abilities in raise local funding for education institutions.

Unbalanced Enrollment Rates

As we can see the enrollment rates are highly correlated with the number of Double First Class Universities in China. This is because the local governments and the universities they fund have an interest in prioritising the access of students with local “hukou” status; The higher the component of municipal finance is in the funding of a university, the higher the bargaining power the local government would have in resisting the national guidelines on opportunity equality.

Elite Capture

Some city/provinces might change, but the the economic performaces or enrollment rates are always high among these cities. Inequalities are instituted and defended by the privileged classes in order to maintain their exclusive hold over scare education resources, which is a source of their privileged status. Education in China may face a state of “elite capture”, where a select elite which benefit from current inequalities in the distribution of education resources use their power to defend their vested interest in maintaining the status quo
